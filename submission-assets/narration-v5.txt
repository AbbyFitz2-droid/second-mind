Most AI begins composing from one prompt. But difficult decisions rarely live
in one message. They live in history: who is involved, what happened before,
what changed, and what remains uncertain.

Second Mind, by Cognisyn, is an evidence-linked cognitive companion built
around one principle: context before composition.

Context can enter naturally—as a message, note, event, or screenshot. The
current prototype files it into an editable relationship graph and a
chronological situation timeline.

In this fictional case, Vargus records five events involving Benedict and
Gertrude. The graph keeps relationships separate, while the timeline preserves
sequence and source.

Merlin does not declare a motive. It tests competing explanations. Here,
romantic tension reaches an illustrative forty-nine percent, while
non-romantic friction remains close at forty-three percent. Every update links
to evidence, discloses its assumptions, and allows the user to downweight,
reject, or correct an inference.

The difference appears at the moment of action. An isolated model responds to
Benedict's request literally. Second Mind offers several context-aware paths
without presenting romance, conflict, or personality as fact. The user
chooses.

Before sending, Perspective Simulation rehearses how Benedict might read the
selected response: its literal meaning, a possible emotional interpretation,
and misunderstanding risk. It models perspectives without claiming access to
someone else’s mind.

We also tested the wider premise—that fuller context can improve
forecasting—on four thousand, one hundred and eighty-eight ConvoKit
conversations, with eight hundred and forty held out and zero paid API calls.
The results are a research baseline, never a tool for judging people.

I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to design, implement, and test the capture pipeline,
relationship graph, epistemic result contract, and evaluation suite. The
optional live reasoning path uses GPT-5.6 through the OpenAI Responses API;
this judge demo remains reproducible without a private key.

Next, the same architecture can support voice capture, Push to Think,
Reflection Mode, and private earbud delivery on existing hardware.

Second Mind does not become the decision-maker. It preserves the evidence,
exposes the inference, and returns the judgment to the person.

Context before composition. Think clearly. Stay yours.
