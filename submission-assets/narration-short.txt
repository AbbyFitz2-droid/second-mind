Most AI assistants see one message. Real conversations have history.
Second Mind is a cognitive companion designed to improve reasoning without
becoming the decision-maker.

The experience begins with natural capture. The user shares a conversation
screenshot instead of maintaining a relationship database by hand. Second
Mind reads it locally and extracts the people, messages, commitments, and
unresolved issues.

Here, Fran arranged a technician, asked for confirmation, and promised to have
a loose tap checked. The screenshot contains a time but no calendar date, so
the date stays unknown. Second Mind never substitutes the upload date.

After one useful clarification, the conversation becomes an editable,
evidence-linked record. Promises and completed actions remain separate.

The relationship graph keeps closeness and trust independent and prevents one
person's history from entering another person's response.

The interpretation view separates direct evidence, possible interpretations,
alternative readings, and uncertainty. AI inference never becomes trusted
memory automatically.

Finally, Second Mind compares an isolated reply with one grounded in the exact
context selected by the user. It notices two open commitments and suggests a
clear next step without claiming motive, deception, or personality. Every
source remains visible and the response remains editable.

I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to build and test the capture pipeline, context
architecture, epistemic result contract, and evaluation suite. The optional
live reasoning path uses GPT-5.6 Terra through the Responses API. This judge
demo uses a reproducible zero-cost mode without a private key.

Second Mind preserves the evidence, exposes the inference, and returns the
decision to the person. Context before composition. Think clearly. Stay yours.
