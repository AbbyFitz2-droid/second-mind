Real conversations have history, but most AI assistants see only one message.
Second Mind is a cognitive companion designed to improve reasoning without
becoming the decision-maker.

The experience begins with natural capture. Instead of asking someone to
maintain a relationship database, the user shares a conversation screenshot.
Second Mind reads the image locally, identifies the people and messages, and
extracts promises, requests, and unresolved issues.

Here, Fran has arranged a technician, asked for confirmation, and promised to
have a loose tap checked. The screenshot contains times but no calendar date,
so Second Mind leaves the date unknown. It never silently substitutes the
upload date.

After one useful clarification, the capture becomes an editable relationship
record. Promises and completed actions remain different states, and each item
stays linked to its source.

The relationship map keeps closeness and trust separate. It also prevents
another person’s history from entering this response accidentally.

The interpretation view separates what was directly said from possible
interpretations, alternative readings, and what is still uncertain. An
AI-generated interpretation never becomes trusted memory automatically.

Finally, Second Mind compares an isolated response with one grounded in the
context the user selected. It can identify two open commitments and suggest a
clear next step without claiming deception, motive, or personality. Every
context item remains visible, and the response stays editable.

I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to build and test the capture pipeline, structured
context architecture, epistemic result contract, and evaluation suite. The
optional live reasoning path uses GPT-5.6 Terra through the Responses API. The
judge demonstration defaults to a reproducible zero-cost mode without a
private API key.

Second Mind preserves the evidence, exposes the inference, and returns the
decision to the person. Context before composition. Think clearly. Stay yours.
